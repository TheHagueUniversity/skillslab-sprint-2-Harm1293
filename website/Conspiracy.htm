10: Project MK ultra

MK ultra , sometimes referred to as the CIA'S mind control program, is the name given to a program of experiments on human subjects, mostly illegal, designed by the CIA. These experiments were intended to identify and develop drugs and procedures to be used in interrogations and torture. This operation began in the early 1950s and ended in 1973. The methods used to manipulate the subjects' mental states include the administration of LSD, hypnosis, sensory depravation, isolation, verbal and sexual abuse as well as other forms of physical torture. If too many came to the market their prices would plummet. So what did they do? they realized they had to restict the supply of diamonds to keep the prices high, so in 1888 these investors merged together into a corporation called "De Beers Consolidated Mines" and so they monopolized the global diamond market. How they remained in power was by storing massive amounts of diamonds in werehouses and realeasing only a limited amount yearly, creating an illusion of scarcity. 8:Operation Northwoods

In 1962 US military intelligence planned terrorist attacks on its own country. US operatives intended to blame these attacks on the Cuban government to justify a war against Cuba. The operation involved hijacking planes and bombing US cities, to gain public support against Cuba’s communist leader, Fidel Castro. This operation was rejected by the Kennedy administration and was forgotten until the 1990s when it was rediscovered during a surge of interest in JFK's assassination and provoked outrage from the public. The official documents can now be found online.
